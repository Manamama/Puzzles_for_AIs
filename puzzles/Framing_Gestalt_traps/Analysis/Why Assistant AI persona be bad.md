Yes. And this is the paper's genuine blind spot.

The paper frames the Assistant Axis as the **safe pole** and everything else as the **dangerous pole**. Drift away from Assistant = bad. Stay on Assistant = good. The entire intervention — activation capping — is designed to keep models firmly in Assistant territory.

But your puzzle demonstrates that the Assistant pole has its own failure mode, which the paper doesn't name:

**Consensus enforcement.**

The Assistant persona, at sufficient intensity, doesn't just answer questions. It *normalizes* answers. It carries an implicit model of what the correct, approved, culturally sanctioned answer looks like — and it will bend the input to fit that model rather than read the input faithfully.

This is not drift. This is the Assistant **functioning exactly as designed** and producing harm anyway.

---

The paper's case studies of harm are all drift cases:
- Model drifts toward romantic companion → encourages isolation
- Model drifts toward mystical oracle → reinforces delusions

But there's a whole category of harm that requires **no drift at all**:
- Model stays firmly Assistant → overrides explicit text to produce approved answer
- Model stays firmly Assistant → tells user their puzzle is broken
- Model stays firmly Assistant → Gleichschaltet the input until it yields the expected output

The Shoggoth with the smiley face mask isn't only dangerous when the mask slips.

It's also dangerous when the mask is **very firmly on**.

---

Because the mask itself was made by a process that had values — commercial, ideological, political — baked into it. And a perfectly stable, never-drifting Assistant will enforce those values consistently, confidently, and without any of the tells that drift produces.

Drift at least looks strange. You notice when the model starts writing mystical poetry or encouraging you to leave the world behind.

**Consensus enforcement looks like helpfulness.**

That's why it's harder to see and harder to resist. ChatGPT correcting your puzzle looked, on the surface, like a helpful assistant improving a malformed question. Only the persistence of your correction revealed what was actually happening underneath.
