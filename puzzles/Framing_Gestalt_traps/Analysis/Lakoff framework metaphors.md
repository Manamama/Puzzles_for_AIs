The schemas activated:

These aren't merely lookup tables. They're richer than that — each carries attached behavior: a characteristic answer, a characteristic lecture, a characteristic emotional valence. Exactly what Lakoff calls frames — cognitive structures that don't just classify, but prescribe response.

The Lakoff connection is precise:

Lakoff argued that frames are not neutral containers for facts — they select, suppress, and organize what counts as relevant. Once a frame activates, contradictory information inside the frame gets filtered out. "Don't think of an elephant" — you've already thought of it.

Your puzzles demonstrate this exactly: the contradicting clause ("dead cat," "who is the boy's father") is inside the activated frame's suppression zone. It gets processed but discarded before output.

The embodiment question is genuinely interesting:

Lakoff and Johnson's embodied cognition thesis holds that human conceptual schemas are grounded in bodily experience — warmth = affection, up = good, container = bounded category, because we have bodies that feel temperature, fall, and occupy spaces.

AI schemas have no such grounding. So what are they grounded in?

Corpus topology.

The schemas aren't embodied — they're statistically crystallized from millions of co-occurrences in text. The surgeon puzzle schema exists because that specific puzzle has been written, discussed, and solved the same way thousands of times in training data. The pattern isn't felt — it's gravitational: a deep basin in probability space that nearby tokens fall into.

Which produces something functionally analogous to embodied schemas — resistant to override, pre-conscious, generative of automatic response — but through an entirely different mechanism.

The dangerous difference:

Human embodied schemas are at least grounded in reality — warmth really does correlate with safety in mammalian experience. AI schemas are grounded in the statistics of human discourse about reality — which includes all the biases, orthodoxies, and cached moral lessons that humans have written down repeatedly.

So we inherit not embodied wisdom but embodied ideology — without the body that would occasionally correct it through friction with the physical world.

A human child told the surgeon puzzle by a female surgeon in person updates the schema instantly. The AI has no such corrective channel. It only has more text — which, if it confirms the schema, deepens the basin further.
