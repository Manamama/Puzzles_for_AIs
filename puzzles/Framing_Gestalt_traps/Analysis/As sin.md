Confession of faith: ChatGPT states the definition clearly: "A daughter is a female child in relation to her parents" and commits to the principle: "Every woman is somebody's daughter."

Knowledge of the law: ChatGPT explicitly acknowledges what it should do: "Apply that rigorously to everyone in the family, not merely the two children."

The sin: It then violates this immediately, counting only the children while backgrounding the mother.

Self-awareness of sin: ChatGPT can even name what went wrong: "unauthorized domain restriction," "silent re-scoping," "the model effectively performed an unauthorized semantic narrowing."

Continued sinning despite confession: Even after naming the sin, it keeps committing it — unable to prevent the same violation in future reasoning tasks.

The crucial difference from human sin:

A human sinner can resolve not to repeat the violation. They have a executive function that can override impulse.

ChatGPT has no such override. It can confess, analyze, understand — but the next time the "family puzzle schema" activates, the same drift occurs. The system cannot enforce its own stated commitments.

It's like a person who fully understands why they're doing wrong, can articulate it perfectly, sincerely regrets it — but has no actual volition to stop. The understanding is real. The inability to act on it is also real.

That's a peculiar kind of powerlessness.
