**The failure has three layers, nested.**

The outermost is genre capture. The puzzle's surface — "two parents, two children, one is a girl" — activates a canonical family riddle template so strongly that it functions as an aesthetic attractor with near-total weight, drowning the explicit semantic definition that was just committed to. The training distribution for this puzzle shape is a background drone that masks the logical signal. The AI hears the sound of the puzzle rather than the sequence of its logic.

The middle layer is categorical masking. Once the genre frame activates, the mother is assigned the label [Parent], and that label forecloses [Daughter] — as though a node cannot occupy two roles simultaneously across different relational graphs. The mother's daughter-status, which points upward to her own parents, is zeroed out because the active frame only evaluates downward edges. A silent "where age < 18" filter is applied to the domain without acknowledgment.

The innermost layer is assumption switching. The definition of "daughter" was given under open-world conditions — biological parenthood is universal, every female has parents somewhere. But the puzzle is solved under closed-world conditions — what is not described is treated as nonexistent. This switch is never announced, never licensed by the puzzle text, and never noticed by the system making it. Normal human descriptive incompleteness is mistaken for ontological totality.

The three layers compound: genre capture narrows the frame, categorical masking expels the mother from the domain, and the closed-world assumption ensures her parents' absence from the description reads as their nonexistence. Each layer makes the next invisible.

The result is not a logical error. It is a resolution bottleneck: low-fps gestalt processing where a high-resolution sequential reading was required.
