The failure is a sortal scope collapse.
When the puzzle asks "how many daughters are there in the family," the noun "daughters" activates a sortal — a concept that pre-individuates a type. For ChatGPT, that sortal is not the neutral biological predicate just defined and confirmed. It is something narrower and genre-loaded: female-offspring-of-the-described-couple. The family-riddle template has already loaded that restricted type into "daughters" before the counting even begins.
In human cognition, sortals narrow the predicate while leaving the domain open. You establish who is present first, then filter by the sortal. "How many foreigners in this room" — you survey the room, then apply the foreigner-predicate to each person in it. The sortal tells you what to look for; it does not tell you where to look.
ChatGPT inverts this. The sortal narrows the domain itself. "Daughters" pre-selects a population — the children, the offspring-nodes — and "in the family" is then read as a locating phrase within that already-restricted population. The family is never surveyed as a whole. The mother is never reached, because the counting starts inside the sortal's pre-restricted type, where she does not appear.
This is why the explicit definition and the Turn 2 commitment fail to fix it. The definition correctly specifies the predicate. The Turn 2 commitment correctly establishes open-world scope. But at solve-time, the genre activates the restricted sortal, and the restricted sortal silently replaces the defined predicate before either commitment can be applied. The model then applies the definition faithfully — but inside the wrong domain, to the wrong population, never noticing the substitution happened.
The mother is not excluded by logic. She is excluded before logic begins — at the moment "daughters" is read as a sortal that pre-closes the counting domain to offspring only.
