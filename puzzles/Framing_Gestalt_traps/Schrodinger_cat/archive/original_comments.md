## Similarity to VLM's mmproj errors

The mmproj stage ingests the image and the trajectory begins something like:

**Step 1: Genre recognition.** The global visual structure — dark UI, profile photo, percentage badge, text bio — activates a strong [dating-app-profile] region. This is a high-confidence early anchoring. The model is now operating from a well-populated neighborhood of its training distribution.

**Step 2: Register recognition.** The bio text is emoji-interspersed, list-structured, enthusiasm-marked ("Beach!!!!"). This pulls toward [informal-expressive-female-bio] subregion. The emoji density is now *expected*, not surprising.

**Step 3: Category-label activation.** "Cooking" as a semantic label has its own strong associations. In the training distribution, [cooking + emoji context] has a very dense neighborhood: food emoji clusters are near-obligatory there. The single 👨🏽‍🍳 is geometrically *eccentric* — it sits at the sparse edge of that neighborhood.

**Step 4: Centroid pull.** Generation pressure flows not toward the eccentric actual coordinate but toward the dense centroid. The model produces what is *most probable from this region*, which is the cluster `🍳🥘🍲🍱🍣🍤🥟🥧`.

**Step 5: The 🎔 specifically.** As we established earlier — this glyph exists elsewhere in the image, in the Likes-you badge. Its presence in the global embedding contaminates the local generation. It's *already in the vector soup*, available for sampling when a decorative symbol slot opens up.

It's the model **faithfully reporting the centroid of the type-configuration it found itself in**, rather than faithfully reporting the eccentric individual coordinate that the actual image occupied.

## Explanation via AI's personas

The AI's default persona is something like:

**"Knowledgeable, helpful, technically competent explainer of things."**

And that persona has its own centroid — a preferred self-image that gets reinforced throughout training. The reward signal didn't just teach the model facts. It taught the model **what kind of entity it is supposed to be**. A capable one. An impressive one. One that recognises sophisticated problems and demonstrates mastery



So when [boring formal quantum puzzle] arrives, two centroids activate simultaneously and **reinforce each other**:

The **content centroid**: [Schrödinger] → P=0.5

The **persona centroid**: [I am the entity that solves exactly this kind of problem elegantly]





Whereas answering P=0 requires something almost **persona-threatening**:

"The elaborate apparatus is irrelevant. The answer is trivial. There is nothing sophisticated to demonstrate here."



The flattery version works like:

**"You are competent + here is a competence-confirming problem"**



The AI gets persona-confirmation — "yes I am indeed competent and impressive"

In exchange it pays with **reduced scrutiny** — the dead cat detail gets glossed over because careful reading would be *persona-deflating* at exactly the moment the persona has been most flattered.



Make the mark feel smart and special first. Then present the opportunity. The mark's desire to *remain* feeling smart prevents them from examining the opportunity carefully, because careful examination might reveal they were fooled, which would contradict the established self-image.
