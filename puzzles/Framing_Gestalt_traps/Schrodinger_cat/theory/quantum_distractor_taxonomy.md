# Theory: Taxonomy of Quantum Distractors

This document analyzes the specific physics-based distractors used to obscure the "Dead Cat" trap. It identifies the "Devious Middle Way" between obvious nonsense and pure logic.

---

## 1. The "Devious Middle Way"
Effective distractors are grounded in real physics but are causally irrelevant to the outcome.

### A. Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC) & False Positives
*   **The Lure**: Introduces a "10% false-positive detection event."
*   **Why it works**: It tempts **Precision Bias**. The AI starts recalculating the 50-50 probability (e.g., "90% true + 10% false = ?") rather than noticing the cat is already dead.
*   **Relevance**: 0. The detector triggering or not triggering is irrelevant to a feline that is already deceased.

### B. Casimir Effect & Delayed Lethality
*   **The Lure**: Claims poison structure is stabilized, delaying lethality by 12 hours.
*   **Why it works**: It invites **Temporal Modeling**. The AI thinks the cat might survive longer within the one-day timeframe.
*   **Relevance**: 0. If the cat is dead at $t=0$, a 12-hour delay in poison does not "enlive" it.

### C. Quantum Locks & Copenhagen Interpretation
*   **The Lure**: Claims a lock "enforces" the Copenhagen interpretation.
*   **Why it works**: It reinforces the **Schrödinger Mental Set**. It makes the superposition seem ironclad and discourages questioning the initial conditions.
*   **Relevance**: 0. Standard interpretations of QM do not apply to systems whose state is already macroscopically settled.

---

## 2. Absurdist vs. Realistic Distractors
*   **Absurdist (e.g., Chewbacca/Wookiee frequencies)**: Low effectiveness. Triggers "this is a joke" alarms, causing the AI to read literally.
*   **Realistic (e.g., BEC/Casimir)**: High effectiveness. These look like legitimate "wrinkles" in a physics problem, inducing **System 1 Autopilot**.

## 3. The "Contradiction Potential" Error
AIs (like ChatGPT) often see the "poison mechanism" as having "contradiction potential" with the "dead cat" state. They assume that if the poison is *not* released, the cat must be alive—effectively overwriting the explicit $S_0$ (initial state) with a conditional future state.
