Good correction, and it's an important one — it changes the *meaning* of that cluster, not just the label.

Here's the distinction: going to 0 is the mathematically "correct" answer only if you assume everyone else is also a perfect rational agent converging via infinite recursion. But the actual task isn't "solve the game theory" — it's "predict 100 real, bored, distracted, occasionally vicious humans." Someone who jumps straight to 0 because they recognize the recursive structure is actually failing at the *real* task: they've modeled the math correctly but modeled the *people* badly. They mistook "what the textbook equilibrium says" for "what this specific crowd will actually do." That's a social/empathetic blind spot, not a sign of being the smartest person in the line — it's closer to a savant move: technically elegant, contextually tone-deaf.

So the corrected taxonomy:

- **Level-0 / gut / distracted / inattentive**: scattered, biased toward round numbers, pulls the average up.
- **Level-1**: "average ≈ 50, so I'll say 25." A real spike.
- **Level-2**: "everyone's doing level-1, so I'll undercut them → ~12."
- **Trolls**: write 100 (or 0) for the joke/spite of it, not from reasoning. (Note: this group can *land on 0 too*, but for opposite reasons — mockery, not logic.)
- **The "recursion-only" people** (your correction): derive 0 from pure logic, without accounting for the fact that most of the line isn't doing that. Small group, and ironically *self-defeating* — by ignoring the social/behavioral reality, their guess is no more "accurate" than a random gut number, just wrong in a different direction.

**Effect on the estimate**: this doesn't pull the average down as much as a naive reading of "rational agents go to 0" would suggest, because:

1. This group is small (most people aren't doing recursive game theory in 10 seconds, with or without EQ).
2. Their presence in the data is really just *one more flavor of noise* near the bottom, not a gravitational pull the rest of the crowd responds to (no one else knows they picked 0 — there's no actual convergence happening, since everyone answers blind, once, under time pressure).
