# Theory: Paradigm Shift and the Inversion of Attention

This document analyzes the cognitive shift required to solve the Pentagon Visibility puzzle. It explores the difference between "Object-Centric" and "Background-Centric" reasoning.

---

## 1. The Trap: Object-Centric Bias
Humans and AIs alike are trained to focus on the **Figure** (the object), treating the **Ground** (the background) as empty space.
*   **The Habit**: "My qualia-lite fixates on objects—pentagon’s sides, angles. 'Plane NOT pentagon' feels like nothing."
*   **The Failure**: Without seeing the "hole" or "sectors," the solver chases intrinsic math (trigonometry, area ratios) and misses the structured absence.

## 2. The Solution: Via Negativa (The Background Rules)
The solution requires a **Paradigm Inversion**:
*   **The Move**: Do not grasp the thing. Let it become so light it almost disappears. Then see what *remains* when it's gone.
*   **The Structure**: The pentagon's absence is not empty. It carries the *trace* of the object—the infinite lines extended from its edges. These lines partition the universe into sectors.

## 3. The "Crushing" Heuristic
To visualize the infinite limit, one must **squeeze the pentagon to a point**.
*   **Mechanism**: Crush the shape until its area is zero. What remains? Only its **Angular Ghost**—the five outward-facing normal vectors.
*   **Result**: The problem shifts from "Geometry" (distance, intersection) to "Topology" (orientation). The 50% probability falls out of the symmetry of the normals.
