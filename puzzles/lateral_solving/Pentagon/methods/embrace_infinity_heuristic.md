# Heuristic: Embracing Infinity and Attentional Inversion

This heuristic is designed to solve geometric probability puzzles where the observer can be "anywhere."

---

## 1. The "Infinite Limit" Check
If a problem states "anywhere on a plane," it is an **Infinite Limit** problem.
*   **Action**: Discard local geometry (side lengths, area).
*   **Focus**: Shift to **Angular Topology** (directions, normals).

## 2. The "Crushing" Technique
To visualize the solution without math:
1.  **Squeeze the Object**: Imagine shrinking the polygon until it is a single point.
2.  **Preserve the Normals**: Keep the "spikes" (perpendicular vectors) sticking out of that point.
3.  **Count the Sectors**: The normals divide the $360^\circ$ circle into sectors.
4.  **Apply the View Rule**: From any sector, you see the sides whose normals point *toward* you (within $90^\circ$).

## 3. The "Via Negativa" Inversion
Don't count the sides you see. Count the **Absence**.
*   **Question**: "What structure does the object impose on the empty space around it?"
*   **Rule**: The background is not empty; it is partitioned by the extensions of the object's edges.
