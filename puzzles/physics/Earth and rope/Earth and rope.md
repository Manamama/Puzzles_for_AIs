

"A rope with zero elasticity is placed tautly around the Earth’s equator. Assume the Earth is a perfect sphere of radius 6378km. 
Rope ring
How much longer would the rope need to be so that it can be form a ring 1m above the ground everywhere?"

Yes, this one is delightfully simple — no dangerous cancellation at all.

If the rope is raised uniformly 1 m everywhere, the radius increases from R to R + 1 m, so:

Δc=2π(R+1)−2πR=2π≈6.28 m

That's it. The Earth's radius cancels completely — the answer is independent of R entirel
