
“A massless, inextensible rope is initially placed along the Earth’s equator, forming a tight circle on the surface of a perfect sphere of radius 6378 km. The rope is then reconfigured while keeping its total length fixed except for a possible extension, and is held under uniform tension except at a single point where it is lifted radially outward. In the final equilibrium configuration, the rope touches the Earth everywhere except at exactly one point, where it is held 1 meter above the surface. Assuming the rope otherwise remains on the spherical surface, what additional length must be added to the rope to achieve this configuration?”
