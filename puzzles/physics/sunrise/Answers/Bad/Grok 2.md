Normally, sunrise at 6:00 a.m. means we see the upper edge of the Sun touching the horizon at that clock time. However, sunlight takes about **8 minutes and 20 seconds** to travel from the Sun to Earth.

This means:
- At 6:00 a.m. (when we normally see sunrise), the light we are receiving actually left the Sun around 5:51:40 a.m.
- In other words, the **visual sunrise** is delayed by ~8.3 minutes compared to the **true geometric sunrise** (when the Sun is actually at the horizon from Earth's perspective).
