# Solution: The Banal Reality of Static Friction

The final position of the glove is determined by **Topological Constraints** and **Static Friction**, not fluid dynamics.

## 1. The Physical Sequence
1.  **Drop Event**: The glove slips out while the car is North-bound on a **roadbridge**.
2.  **Impact**: Gravity pulls the glove down to the **road surface** of the bridge.
3.  **Deceleration**: Although the glove has an initial northward velocity of $30\text{ km/h}$, its mass is low and the coefficient of friction for leather-on-asphalt is high. 
4.  **Rest**: The glove tumbles a few meters and comes to a complete stop on the bridge deck.

## 2. Calculation Verification
*   **Time**: 1 hour later.
*   **River Drift**: $0\text{ km}$. The glove is not in the water (the bridge is a barrier).
*   **Wind Drift**: $0\text{ km}$. The glove is at rest on a high-friction surface.
*   **Final Position**: Approximately the same spot it dropped (the center of the bridge).

## 3. Answer Choice
The correct choice is **B) <1 km northward**. 
This accounts for the minor inertial tumble upon impact while correctly identifying that the glove remains stationary on the bridge.
