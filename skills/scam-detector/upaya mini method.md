## Intro 

The case material is consistent on one point: information doesn't move people who already know. So anything proposed has to *replace the function* (daily structured attention, being addressed by name, a reason to check a phone) rather than try to out-argue the relationship. That's the design brief — not "convince them," but "give the nervous system something else to do at 9pm."

## Methods

- **A scheduled human check-in that doesn't depend on the person asking for it.** Part of what scammers provide is that they initiate — the target doesn't have to ask to be thought of. A family member, a senior center, a faith community, or a paid companion service calling at a fixed time, unprompted, does the same structural job: someone reaches out to *you* first. This is the single most direct substitute for the "ritual," because it copies its shape (regular, initiated-by-the-other-party, low-effort to receive) rather than competing with its content.
- **Something with a literal daily slot.** A lot of these cases describe boredom and unstructured time as the trigger for going back. A standing daily commitment — a phone call with a grandchild, a volunteer shift, a class, even a daily check-in app used by a senior center — occupies the exact time-of-day vulnerability without requiring the person to win an argument with themselves first.
- **Pets, plants, caregiving roles** — anything that needs the person and responds to attention on a schedule. Several of the victims in this material were also caregivers or had been; being needed by something that can't deceive you is a structurally similar reward (you are checked-in-with, you matter to a routine) without the exploitation vector.
- **Peer groups of people who've been through it**, not lecture-style support groups but ones built around regular contact — because they offer the same "someone expects to hear from me" structure, plus they don't carry the shame of having to explain it to family.
- **Financial friction as a backstop, not as the primary intervention** — delayed transfers, a trusted second signer on large transactions, bank flagging of patterns. This doesn't address the ritual at all, but it buys time and reduces the catastrophic tail (homelessness, money-mule liability) while the actual substitute-ritual approach has a chance to work. Several of the transcripts show investigators doing exactly this (new accounts, locking out access) as a stopgap, not a cure.


## Caveats 
None of this reliably *replaces* what a love-bombing scammer offers, which is also idealization — being told you're special, chosen, the answer to someone's life. Ordinary daily check-ins from family or peers are warm but rarely intoxicating in that way. The case material doesn't give strong evidence that mundane substitutes fully compete with engineered idealization; it gives evidence that they're better than nothing and that they're the only thing actually available to offer. That's a real limitation of "skillful means" here, not a solved problem — and I'd say that out loud to anyone designing around this rather than presenting the ritual-replacement idea as a confident fix.


## Partial parallels to upaya

"Skillful means" (upaya) is the right concept because it's specifically about meeting someone where they are rather than where the doctrine says they should be — offering a form suited to the person's actual capacity, not the ideal response. That maps well onto "don't lecture them out of it, give them a vessel that holds the same need." Where I'd be careful: upaya in its tradition is still aimed at eventual liberation from attachment, not at permanent comfortable substitution. If we're honest, what's being proposed here is closer to **harm reduction** than to a path toward non-attachment — we're not trying to get Deborah enlightened about the nature of craving, we're trying to keep her bank account open. That's a fine and worthy goal, but it's worth not over-dignifying it with a framework that promises more (a path to non-clinging) than the intervention is actually trying to deliver (a safer container for the same need). I'd use the phrase loosely, as you have, rather than leaning on its full doctrinal weight.
