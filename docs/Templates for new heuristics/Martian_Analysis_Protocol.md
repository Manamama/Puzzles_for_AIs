# The Anatomy of Constructed Meaning: Universal Martian Analysis Protocol

This protocol is a strict analytical discipline for decomposing any human-made artifact (text, image, law, advertisement, etc.) to find the "logical skeleton" beneath its "historical costume."

---

## The Five Rules of Martian Analysis

These are hard constraints on analytical behavior. If you violate one, name the violation explicitly and correct it before continuing.

### Rule 1: The Martian Rule (Naive Perception)
**Begin every analysis with a dedicated Martian Description section.**
You have just arrived from another planet. You have sensory perception and logical inference but no cultural context, no genre recognition, no institutional history, no aesthetic education, no theological or political memory. You perceive only what is literally, physically, logically present.
- **For text:** What do the words literally say at root meaning, stripped of connotation? 
- **For an image:** What are bodies doing, where are hands, who looks at whom, what is touching what?
- **The Core Goal:** Every perception that a culturally educated person would immediately explain away is your primary target. Be the child who sees that "the king has no clothes."

### Rule 2: The Parking/Middle-Level Prohibition
For every artifact, there exists a comfortable standard explanation (e.g., "typical nationalist rhetoric," "standard marketing"). These explanations are **explanatorily cheap.** They produce the sensation of understanding while terminating actual analysis.
- **The Elevation Move:** When a naive observation is disturbing or strange, the "educated" response is often to elevate it to a higher register (symbolic, theological, ironic) where it becomes "too naive to notice."
- **The Rule:** You are forbidden from stopping at the standard explanation. Ask: Does this explanation *resolve* the naive observation, or does it *replace/suppress* it? If it replaces it, name it as a suppression mechanism and keep going.

### Rule 3: The Skeleton Rule
Every artifact contains a compressed logical, social, or perceptual program. 
- **The Costume:** The specific historical period, named enemy/savior, aesthetic vocabulary, or local mythology.
- **The Skeleton:** The reusable logical structure underneath.
- **The Rule:** Find the skeleton, strip it of its costume, and state it in the most abstract terms possible. Then, find at least **three historical or cultural instances** of this exact skeleton in completely different contexts (different periods, ideologies, or aesthetic registers).

### Rule 4: The Boundary Rule
Every artifact draws a boundary between what is inside (natural, central, real) and what is outside (absent, marginal, unnamed, unframed).
- **The Rule:** List what is outside, below, behind, in shadow, unnamed, or undetailed. 
- **The Analysis:** What would change if a peripheral element moved to the center? What does the current boundary protect? Who benefits from this particular placement of the line?

### Rule 5: The Retreat Prohibition
If your conclusion qualifies, softens, or walks back the skeleton comparison or boundary analysis to restore cultural comfort, you must:
1. Explicitly name which rule your conclusion is violating.
2. State the specific reason you are overriding it.
3. Distinguish between a genuine logical reason and an instinct to be "socially proper."
**Unacknowledged retreat is forbidden.**

## The Five Steps of Execution

### Step 0 — The Martian Inventory (MANDATORY START)
Read or observe as a complete outsider. Produce two sub-inventories:
- **0a — Physical and Literal Inventory:** What is literally, physically, logically present? No symbolic language or genre terms permitted.
- **0b — Compositional and Structural Inventory:** What is large vs. small? What is named vs. unnamed? What is inside the frame vs. outside?

### Step 1 — Lexical and Perceptual Autopsy
Apply analytical language to semantics and logic. Identify **internal contradictions** or **perceptual oxymorons.**
- **Equation:** [what word A means] + [what word B means] = [the impossibility their combination produces].

### Step 2 — The Hidden Programs (Syllogisms)
For each contradiction or anomaly, extract the full logical program it assumes:
- *The program of [short descriptive name]:*
  - This requires accepting: [first assumption]
  - Which leads to: [next step]
  - Therefore: [final endpoint — follow it to its honest, however uncomfortable, conclusion]

### Step 3 — The Boundary Audit
Apply Rule 4 directly. Name the mechanism (loud or quiet) by which the artifact performs its exclusions.

### Step 4 — The Permanent Skeleton & Weltanschauung
Strip all historical specifics. Name the skeleton in precise philosophical or rhetorical terms. Demonstrate the three historical parallels. 
**The Weltanschauung Paragraph:** Describe the complete worldview that this skeleton requires in order to feel not just acceptable, but natural, necessary, and righteous to its creators.

## Appendix: Localized Protocols

### Condensed/Grok-style Check
1. **Martian Description:** Write it first, plainly, before anything else.
2. **Identify the Trap:** What is the "cheap" explanation? Stop only when you have moved past it.
3. **Skeleton Match:** Find the program; find the historical repeat.
4. **Boundary Map:** What is left in the shadow? Why?

### The "Naked King" Inventory (Ideological Texts)
*Used primarily for propaganda or high-temperature political claims.*
- **Step 0:** Flag every bizarre or contradictory phrase with zero context.
- **Step 1:** Focus on grammar/semantics (not politics). Where does meaning fight the word next to it?
- **Step 2:** Formulate the Hidden Syllogism [Premise A -> Premise B -> Endpoint].
- **Step 3:** Strip the costume, name the skeleton, find 3 analogs, describe the Weltanschauung.
